Recycling Guide

Knowing what goes where is half the battle. Use this guide to understand how to handle complex household waste.

🔋

Electronics

E-waste contains hazardous materials like lead and mercury, but also valuable resources like gold and copper. Never place e-waste in your standard kerbside bins.

Practical Tips:

  • Wipe personal data from devices before recycling.
  • Tape battery terminals to prevent fire risks.
  • Remove batteries if easily detachable.
🛍️

Soft Plastics

If you can scrunch it into a ball, it’s soft plastic. These jam automated sorting machines at standard recycling facilities, forcing shutdowns.

Practical Tips:

  • Ensure plastics are thoroughly clean and dry.
  • Cut off any rigid plastic zippers or spouts.
  • Collect in a designated bin before drop-off.
🍎

Organic Composting

Food organics break down in landfill without oxygen, releasing methane—a potent greenhouse gas. Composting returns nutrients to the soil.

Practical Tips:

  • Line your caddy with certified compostable bags.
  • Include crushed eggshells and coffee grounds.
  • Avoid large bones and excessive fats/oils.
🍾

Glass Recycling

Glass is infinitely recyclable without losing quality. However, broken glass in standard bins can contaminate other recyclable materials like paper.

Practical Tips:

  • Rinse bottles and jars to remove food residue.
  • Remove metal/plastic lids (recycle separately).
  • Never include drinking glasses or window glass.
📦

Paper & Cardboard

One of the most widely recycled materials, paper fibers can be recycled up to seven times. Contamination from food or grease ruins the recycling batch.

Practical Tips:

  • Flatten all cardboard boxes to save bin space.
  • Tear off greasy parts of pizza boxes.
  • Avoid coated papers (like glossy gift wrap).
☢️

Hazardous Waste

Chemicals, paints, and fluorescent tubes pose severe risks to the environment and sanitation workers if disposed of incorrectly.

Practical Tips:

  • Keep chemicals in original, labelled containers.
  • Never mix different chemicals together.
  • Wait for council-run chemical drop-off events.
