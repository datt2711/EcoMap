Beyond the Bin

Understanding our waste footprint is the first step toward a circular economy. Let's look at the numbers behind Australia's recycling habits.

Recovery vs. Landfill

While Australia is making significant strides in waste recovery, millions of tonnes still end up in landfill. The gap between generation and recovery represents lost resources that could be reintroduced into the economy.

By utilising specialised drop-off locations (like those found on EcoMap), we can directly influence this recovery rate, diverting complex materials from landfill.

Waste Composition

A significant portion of our household waste consists of organics and paper/cardboard. Interestingly, organics placed in standard landfill bins decompose anaerobically, creating methane.

Diverting food organics and garden organics (FOGO) into dedicated composting facilities is one of the most impactful climate actions a household can take.
